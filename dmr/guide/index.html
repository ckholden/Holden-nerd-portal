DMR PTT

A browser walkie-talkie on the family TGIF talkgroup (HOLDEN1) — no hotspot or radio needed, works from your phone or a desktop browser.

  1. Sign in — Google sign-in, or "Email me a sign-in link" for AOL/Yahoo/any address. First time signing in, you'll request access (callsign + DMR ID) and Christian gets a push to approve it.
  2. Tap 🔌 Connect — required first step, opens your mic and the connection. (Re-appears as "▶ Resume monitoring" after you stop.)
  3. Pick a network — really 2 networks: TGIF (home base — HOLDEN1 + curated talkgroups) and BrandMeister, which itself has 2 tabs: the main BrandMeister tab, and BM · Oregon — a second bridge into a different BrandMeister master carrying Oregon 1 + PNW Regional. Both BrandMeister tabs start locked until you set a BrandMeister password in Settings (see below).
  4. Browse or search talkgroups — tap "All talkgroups" to expand the list, or type in the search box. Tap a talkgroup name to tune to it. Tap ☆ to star a favorite for quick access.
  5. Talk — hold the big PTT button (or hold Space on desktop — it's press-and-hold, not a tap). Release to listen. Settings lets you switch to tap-to-toggle instead of hold.
  6. Leave it open to monitor, or tap "Stop monitoring" to go quiet.
Install it — a real installed app, not just a pinned tab: tap 📲 Install app (Android and desktop Chrome/Edge show this automatically) for a proper home-screen/taskbar app. On iPhone, Safari doesn't support that button, so use Share ↑ then "Add to Home Screen" instead — and note iPhone requires that install step before push alerts will work at all.

Settings (gear icon): PTT mode, transmit time-out, permit tone / roger beep, keep screen awake, headset mode (louder, no echo cancel), mic & speaker device pickers, mic level, 🔔 Call alerts (turns on push notifications) and, separately, 📵 Away alerts (whether those pushes fire while you're away — on by default), and the BrandMeister password field that unlocks both BM tabs.

Handy links just above the PTT button: Radio Bulletins and a jump straight to DMR Monitor.

Codeplug Search

Search the shared AnyTone 878 template by channel name, frequency, talkgroup, or zone name — find out exactly where something lives before you go looking for it on the radio.

  • Type anything in the search box — channel name (PB OR1), a frequency (440.9375), a talkgroup (TAC 310 or its number), or a zone name.
  • Each result shows the mode (DMR/FM badge), RX/TX frequencies, and every zone the channel belongs to — plus its position number in that zone, e.g. DMR POWELLBTT (#1). AnyTone radios list a zone's channels in that exact order, so the number tells you where to scroll to on the actual radio. (Talkgroup column only applies to DMR rows — analog/FM channels show a dash there.)
  • Results cap at the first 500 matches with a "refine your search" note — a very broad search term (like a single letter) may get truncated.

DMR Monitor

Live "who's talking" across the whole PNWDigital network, the home hotspot, and BrandMeister — no need to be on the radio yourself to see what's active. Each of you gets your own identity and logbook when signed in.

  • Source tabs — 🌐 PNW Network (~8 sec refresh) / 🏠 Hotspot (~5 sec refresh) / 📡 BrandMeister (live feed, no fixed rate) / All.
  • Search + filters — search box filters by callsign, name, city, talkgroup, or repeater. "All talkgroups"/"All repeaters" open checkbox panels that support picking several at once, not just one — all filters combine together.
  • Talkgroups · last 60 min strip up top — tap a chip to jump straight to that talkgroup's traffic.
  • Map — tap 🗺 to see station locations; toggle the repeater layer, base map style, fit-to-activity, flip between repeater-site and operator-home placement, or pop the map into its own window.
  • Tap a station to log a QSO into your own logbook — unless a net is currently being hosted, in which case tapping instead checks that station into the net roster.

Also available: a Net Control mode for running/logging a live net (owner use), a Top Talkgroups leaderboard (Today/7-day/30-day/all-time), a one-tap "Listen" link that jumps a BrandMeister talkgroup into DMR PTT, and ADIF export of your logbook from Settings.

The basics

Your radio does two things: ANALOG (FM) — regular ham repeaters & simplex — and DMR (digital) — the PNWDigital network, where your local repeater is linked to repeaters across Oregon/Washington so you can talk a long way, not just line-of-sight. Channels are grouped into zones (think: folders) like "DMR POWELLBTT" or "HAM CROOK CO".

Calling family on DMR

  1. Everyone tunes to their OR1 channel (e.g. Pete: PB OR1, Chris: CORB OR1) — statewide calling talkgroup.
  2. Call: "KK7RBQ, this is KJ7DTS, on Oregon 1, Powell Butte."
  3. They answer with their callsign.
  4. Move off the shared calling channel: "Let's go to TAC 310." Both switch to the TAC310 channel (e.g. PB TAC310 / CORB TAC310).
  5. Re-connect and chat as long as you like — ID with your callsign at the start, end, and every ~10 min.

Analog works the same way but with no switching — call and chat right on the one repeater channel.

Understanding DMR talkgroups

Picture a DMR repeater as a highway with two lanes (timeslots). Each lane carries different conversations (talkgroups) — "Local 1", "Oregon 1", "PNW 1", etc. Your channel is parked on one talkgroup. The #1 gotcha: you can hear someone whose talkgroup is different from the one your channel is set to — if you key up, they won't hear you. Fix: check what talkgroup the screen shows for the incoming call, switch your channel to match (channel = talkgroup here), then key up.

Network etiquette

  • Listen first — make sure the talkgroup is quiet before you call.
  • Regional talkgroups (OR1/PNW1) reach across the whole state — announce your repeater so people can place you, then move to a TAC channel to actually visit.
  • Leave a ~2 second gap after someone stops before replying.
  • Don't key talkgroup 4000 — that's a hotspot/BrandMeister disconnect command, does nothing useful on PNWDigital.

Troubleshooting

  • I hear them but they can't hear me — you're on a different talkgroup than they are. Switch to match (see above).
  • No beep when I key / can't transmit — wrong zone/channel, or a listen-only channel (some public-safety/weather channels are receive-only).
  • Is my radio even working? — go to PARROT, talk 3 seconds, listen for the echo.
  • I'm totally lost — your home DMR zone, channel OR1. That's family-net home base.
  • Still stuck — call Christian.
